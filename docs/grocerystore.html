At the grocery store

Bruno drives to the grocery store. Once there, however, he is confounded again: there are far too many (but still finite) aisles! An employee approaches him. "To save ink, we've replaced all of our signs with regular expressions. Just check your items against them to figure out which aisle to use. We trust this will be much faster for you."

Bruno looks at the first item on his list: "Lasagne paasta." (Apparently he made a few typos while creating the list.) He looks at a few of the aisles:

  • Aisle 0: L(a|s)(a|s)(gne)* p(aa)*(s|a) ta
  • Aisle 1: (La)(a|s|a)(g)*ne (pasta)*
  • Aisle 2: L(a|s|a)(a|s|a)*gn(e|a) pa(a)*sta( noodles|)
  • Aisle 3: L(a|s)(a|gne)*pa(a)sta
